This site outlines and begins an ongoing project. Namely the components of File Submission and Subject Categorization. Purpose: Optimizing the storage and retrieval of collected files, including:

  • ~10,000 e-books
  • ~500 Social Science Documents
  • ~500 Natural Science Documents
  • ~5,000 Formal Science Documents
  • ~1,000 Unsorted/Misc. Documents

These are all stored within a conventional file system. For instance

../Formal Sciences/Computer Science/Programming Languages/Java/Swing

For many files this is appropriate. What about in the case of "Beginning Ethical Hacking with Python", destination folders of '../Python' and '../PenTest' are both valid candidates. A small scale solution could be to simply place a copy of the file in to each folder. However, this scales very poorly. So, why not borrow from the RDB system? In some respects, my goal is to emulate a tagging system. Any file could be entered in to the system (see sample form link on left). In MS Access, records may have an 'attachment' field type. This could be utilized as a bridge between the FMS and RDBMS. Records would be given 1+ 'Subject' attribute. To begin defining what constitutes a 'Subject', the super ultimate source of Wikipedia was used. Before this project was an idea, there were multiple occasions where I simply didn't know what a files Subject might even be:

Magnetic Tornadoes as Energy Channels Into the Solar Corona

Not to long after did I query Google for an image of The Fields of the Branches of Science; nothing. At least, nothing as detailed as I was hoping for. Thus began my work of working through various wikis, building rough models of what I had searched for (see Branches of Science > Physical Sciences on Left). Ideally, the subject fields will be customizable by the user as needed, but the end result of these models should provide a quality default build. Below are a couple of images which demonstrate the complexities of mentally mapping a file system through scaling. The proposed system could negate this, a user only needing to enter the desired subject field(s) to retrieve all relative files, regardless of location on the system. Once this much is complete, I can start describing the visual aspect..
